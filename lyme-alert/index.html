Lyme Alert FAQ

Lyme Disease, Tick Bite, and Rash Awareness

Am I in a weekly Lyme alert area?

A weekly Lyme alert means that GEBA GeoAI and TrendAI signals suggest increased local awareness may be useful. If your county appears in a high-alert area, take extra care with tick bite prevention, tick checks, and outdoor exposure.

What should I do if I have symptoms after a tick bite?

If you are in a Lyme alert area and develop an expanding rash, bullseye-like rash, fever, fatigue, headache, muscle aches, or joint pain after a tick bite, seek medical care promptly.

Why do blacklegged ticks matter?

Lyme disease in many U.S. regions is linked to infected blacklegged ticks, also called deer ticks. Checking your body, clothing, children, and pets after outdoor activity can help reduce risk.

How should I use this page?

Use this weekly Lyme alert to check the map, review high-alert counties, monitor possible rash or symptoms, and connect to trusted health resources when needed. This Lyme alerts are for awareness only and are not medical diagnosis or treatment advice.
